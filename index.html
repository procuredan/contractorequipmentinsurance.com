Why Contractors Need Equipment Insurance

Contractor equipment is exposed to theft, vandalism, rollover accidents, transport damage, fires, and jobsite losses every day. Standard commercial auto or general liability policies often do not fully protect contractor equipment and mobile machinery.

Inland marine insurance and contractor equipment coverage can help protect excavators, skid steers, bulldozers, loaders, trailers, and tools whether they are on the jobsite, being transported, or temporarily stored.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does contractor equipment insurance cover theft?

Many contractor equipment policies provide protection against theft and vandalism depending on the policy structure and coverage selected.

What is inland marine insurance?

Inland marine insurance helps protect contractor tools and equipment while being transported, stored, or used away from a fixed location.

Can rented equipment be insured?

Yes. Many contractors add coverage for rented or leased equipment to help protect against expensive losses.

Does equipment insurance cover transport damage?

Coverage may apply while equipment is being transported depending on policy terms and conditions.

Contractor Equipment Insurance Coverage

Contractor equipment insurance helps protect construction equipment, mobile machinery, tools, trailers, and heavy equipment used by contractors, excavation companies, utility contractors, and landscaping businesses.

Policies can often be customized to help cover equipment against theft, vandalism, collision damage, fire, rollover losses, transport damage, and certain weather-related claims.

Coverage may apply to excavators, skid steers, bulldozers, compact track loaders, dump trailers, trenchers, generators, compressors, and other contractor equipment used on jobsites and while in transit.
